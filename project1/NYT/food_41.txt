The Fresher the Spice, the Better the Chicken Paprikash
It was Christopher Columbus who brought the pepper to Europe, a souvenir of his travels in the New World, and the Ottoman Empire that brought it to Eastern Europe, dried and ground fine into crimson spice. Hungarians in particular went mad for the stuff, stirring the seasoning into their theretofore-plain stews and fricassees: paprika, they called it, from the Latin piper, or "pepper."
