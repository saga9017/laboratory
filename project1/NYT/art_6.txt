Review: 'Frankenstein,' at Royal Ballet, Complete With Dissection
There is one thing to be said for Liam Scarlett's new "Frankenstein," a lavish costume-and-sets affair at the Royal Ballet that had its premiere at the Royal Opera House here on Wednesday night. It quite possibly has the first dissection scene in a ballet, in which a corpse is dismembered and merry students dance joyously with severed arms and legs.
